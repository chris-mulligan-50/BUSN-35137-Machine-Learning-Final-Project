Final Project
Business 35137
Winter 2026
A major component of the evaluation for this course is a final project. The project can be
completed in groups of up to four students. The goal of this project is to give you an opportunity to
get your hands dirty with real data and to apply the tools and techniques we have learned in class
to a real-world problem of your choosing in finance. The project will be broken down into three
parts: a proposal, a write-up, and a presentation. Overall, the project will be worth 40% of your
final grade; 10% for the proposal, 10% for the write-up, and 20% for the presentation.
1.Proposal (Due 2/2): The proposal should be a brief (1-2 page) description of the project
you plan to undertake. You should make clear what the research question is, what data you
plan to use, and what methods you plan to apply.
2.W rite-up (Due 3/2): The write-up should be a more detailed (5-10 page) description of your
findings. Explain the research question, methods used, data, and results. Null results are fine,
but you should still explain what you did and why you think you got null results.
3.Presentation: The group presentations will take place the last two weeks of class. Each group
will be given 20 minutes to present their project.
